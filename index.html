Porsche 911

The Porsche 911 (pronounced Nine Eleven or Neunelfer in German) is a two-door 2+2 high performance rear-engined sports car introduced by Porsche AG of Stuttgart, Germany in September 1964. It was originally equipped with a torsion bar suspension and a rear-mounted flat-six engine. The 911 has been extensively raced in a variety of classes by both private and factory teams. It is one of the most successful competition vehicles. The naturally aspirated 911 Carrera RSR won world championship races such as the Targa Florio and the 24 Hours of Daytona in the mid-1970s. In 1979, the 911-derived 935 turbo won the 24 Hours of Le Mans. Porsche won the World Championship for Makes with 911-derived models in 1976, 1977, 1978, and 1979.
